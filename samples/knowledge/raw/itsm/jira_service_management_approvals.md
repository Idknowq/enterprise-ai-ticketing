# What are approvals?

Some requests might need approval before they can move to the next status in their workflow. For example:

- Requests for new system accounts may need to be approved by an IT manager.

- A cell phone purchase over a certain value might need to be approved by a group of people that might include someone from Information Security, someone from IT, and a Finance manager.

- Leave requests may need to be approved by an employee’s direct manager.

Approvers don't need a Jira Service Management license to approve requests – they just have to be customers of the service space . Learn more about users and roles in Jira Service Management .

You can add an approval step to any status in any request type’s workflow. Learn more about workflows .

### How approvals work

Once you’ve added an approval step to a workflow status:

- A person creates a Jira Service Management request.

- The request transitions to a status that has an approval step.

- The designated approvers are notified by email that they need to approve or decline the request.

- The approvers approve or decline the request, either from the help center, directly from the email, or in Slack or Microsoft Teams .

- The request transitions to the next status in the workflow, and the person who submitted the request is notified of any comments that the approver added.
